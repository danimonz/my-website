Mexican Rice

Ingredients

  • 1 cup white rice (medium grain)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cups chicken broth or water
  • 1 medium tomato (about ¾ cup chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons white onion, chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional:
  • 1 small carrot, finely chopped
  • ½ cup peas
  • 1 sprig cilantro
  • 1 serrano pepper

Instructions

  1. Rinse and Prepare the Rice:
    1. Place the rice in a large heat-proof bowl and cover with hot water.
    2. Transfer to a strainer and rinse under cold water. Drain well and shake off excess water. Set aside to dry slightly.
  2. Prepare the Peas:
    1. If using frozen peas, defrost them ahead of time. Otherwise, rinse and set aside.
  3. Fry the Rice:
    1. Heat the oil in a large or medium saucepan.
    2. Add the rice and cook over medium heat, stirring with a wooden spoon, until it turns a delicate golden brown.
    3. Carefully tilt the pan and remove excess oil with a spoon.
  4. Puree the Tomato Mixture:
    1. Place the tomatoes, garlic, and onion in a blender or food processor. Blend until smooth.
  5. Add Tomato Mixture to the Rice:
    1. Add the blended mixture to the rice and stir over medium-high heat.
    2. Cook until the liquid is absorbed (about 3 minutes), making sure it does not stick to the bottom.
  6. Cook the Rice:
    1. Add the broth and optional vegetables (carrot, peas, serrano, cilantro). Season with salt.
    2. Bring to a boil (about 2 minutes), then cover and reduce heat to low.
    3. Cook 12–15 minutes until liquid is fully absorbed.
  7. Fluff and Serve:
    1. Check for remaining moisture. If needed, cook a few more minutes on low, covered.
    2. Remove from heat and let stand, covered, for 10–15 minutes to steam.
    3. Fluff gently with a fork and serve.

Notes

Some cooks in Mexico avoid stirring the rice while it cooks to prevent it from becoming mushy.
