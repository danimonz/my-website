Oatmeal Cookies

Ingredients

  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ⅓ cup packed light brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ¼ teaspoon baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt (use a pinch if using salted butter)
  • ¾ cup old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • Flaky sea salt, for finishing

Instructions

  1. Cream the butter and sugars: In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened butter with the granulated sugar and brown sugar until light and fluffy. Add the egg and vanilla; mix until smooth.
  2. Add the dry ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Add this mixture to the wet ingredients and mix on low just until a soft dough forms.
  3. Fold in the oats and chocolate: Stir in the oats, then fold in the chocolate chips.
  4. Chill: Cover and refrigerate the dough for at least 1 hour to help control spreading and create those thin, crisp edges.
  5. Bake: Preheat the oven to 350°F. Scoop rounded tablespoons of dough, roll into balls, and place them on a parchment-lined baking sheet, leaving several inches between each one (they spread quite a bit). Bake 12–15 minutes, or until the edges are deeply golden and the centers still look slightly soft.
  6. Finish with sea salt: While the cookies are warm on the baking sheet, sprinkle a small pinch of flaky sea salt on top of each one. Let them rest for a minute before transferring them to a cooling rack.
  7. Cool: The cookies will crisp as they cool completely.
